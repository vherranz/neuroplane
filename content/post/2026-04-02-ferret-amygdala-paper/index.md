We are pleased to share our new article published in *Journal of Comparative Neurology*.

In this study, we identify immature excitatory neurons in the postnatal ferret amygdala and compare their organization with other mammalian species. The work shows that immature neurons extend into the amygdala not only in ferret, but also in marmoset and swine, supporting the idea that protracted maturation of paralaminar neurons is a conserved mammalian feature rather than a primate-specific one. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0} :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

This work was carried out in collaboration with the laboratories of Shawn Sorrells at the University of Pittsburgh and Arturo Alvarez-Buylla at UCSF. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

We are especially proud that the first author of the paper is Lucía Inés Torrijos-Saiz. Her PhD thesis, defended in July 2025, included a substantial part of the results presented in this article.

**Reference**  
Torrijos-Saiz LI, Ghibaudi M, Sharief M, Ljungqvist Brinson L, Alvarez-Buylla A, García-Verdugo JM, Herranz-Pérez V, Sorrells S. *Immature Excitatory Neurons in the Postnatal Ferret Paralaminar Nuclei and Their Relationship to the Amygdala Across Species*. *Journal of Comparative Neurology* (2026).
